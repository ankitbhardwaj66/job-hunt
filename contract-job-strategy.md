# Contract Job Hunting Strategy

## Strategy 1: LinkedIn Direct Outreach to Small Tech Companies

**Target:** CEOs, CTOs, Tech Leads of companies with ~50 or fewer employees.

**How to find them:**
- Search LinkedIn for companies by size filter (1-50 employees), industry (Technology)
- Look at "People" tab of those companies and identify decision-makers
- Engage with their posts before cold messaging (like, comment for a week or two)

---

### Message Templates

#### Tone 1: Warm & Personal

> Hey [Name],
>
> Stumbled on [Company Name] and really liked what you guys are doing with [specific area] — had to reach out.
>
> So I'm a dev who does contract work with small teams. Full-stack, cloud stuff, DevOps — basically whatever needs building or fixing. I know smaller teams don't always need a full-time hire but could use an extra hand now and then.
>
> You can check out some of my work here: ankitbhardwaj.in
>
> Anyway, no agenda here — just thought I'd say hi in case you ever need someone. Happy to chat anytime!
>
> Cheers,
> Ankit

#### Tone 2: Casual & Friendly

> Hey [Name],
>
> Been following [Company Name] for a bit — really cool what you're building!
>
> Quick intro — I'm a dev who works with small teams on a contract basis. Need someone to knock out a feature backlog, clean up tech debt, set up infra, or just be an extra pair of hands during a crunch? That's kinda my thing.
>
> Here's my site if you wanna see what I've been up to: ankitbhardwaj.in
>
> No pressure at all — just putting myself out there. Would love to connect either way!
>
> Cheers,
> Ankit

#### Tone 3: Value-First & Consultative

> Hey [Name],
>
> Saw [Company Name] is growing — that's awesome! I know scaling tech with a small team can get hectic real fast.
>
> I do contract work with companies like yours — shipping features, cleaning up code, setting up pipelines, filling gaps when you need someone who can just jump in and get stuff done. No long onboarding, no overhead.
>
> A few things I've helped teams with recently:
> - [Example: Migrated a monolith to microservices]
> - [Example: Built CI/CD pipelines from scratch]
> - [Example: Shipped a customer-facing feature in 3 weeks]
>
> Here's my site for more context: ankitbhardwaj.in
>
> If any of that sounds useful, happy to hop on a quick call — totally informal, just a chat.
>
> Cheers,
> Ankit

## Additional Strategies

### Strategy 2: Freelance Platforms (Targeted, Not Spray-and-Pray)

- **Toptal** — vetted network, higher-quality contracts, premium rates
- **Upwork** — filter for longer-term contracts; build a strong profile with a few initial gigs
- **Gun.io** — specifically for freelance developers
- **Arc.dev** — remote developer marketplace
- **Contra** — commission-free freelance platform

**Tip:** Don't compete on price. Write proposals that show you understand the client's problem, not just list your skills.

### Strategy 3: Niche Job Boards for Contract/Remote Work

- **We Work Remotely** (weworkremotely.com)
- **Remote OK** (remoteok.com)
- **Working Nomads** (workingnomads.com)
- **FlexJobs** (flexjobs.com)
- **Wellfound / AngelList** — filter for contract roles at startups

### Strategy 4: Open Source & Public Building

- Contribute to open source projects used by small companies
- Write technical blog posts or tutorials (Dev.to, Hashnode, Medium)
- Build small tools or side projects publicly on GitHub
- This builds credibility and creates inbound leads over time

### Strategy 5: Tech Communities & Slack Groups

- Join Slack/Discord communities where startup founders hang out:
  - Indie Hackers
  - r/startups, r/SaaS communities
  - Local tech Slack groups (city-specific)
  - Niche communities (e.g., DevOps, React, etc.)
- Offer help, answer questions — people will reach out when they need contract work

### Strategy 6: Agency & Recruiter Partnerships

- Connect with staffing agencies that specialize in contract tech roles:
  - Robert Half Technology
  - TEKsystems
  - Hays Technology
- Let recruiters know you're specifically looking for contract/part-time work
- They often have clients who need short-term help but don't post publicly

### Strategy 7: Referral Network Activation

- Tell your existing professional network (ex-colleagues, friends in tech) that you're available for contract work
- A simple LinkedIn post: *"Hey network — I'm taking on contract projects. If your team needs short-term dev help, let's talk."*
- Referrals convert at a much higher rate than cold outreach

### Strategy 8: Cold Email to Startups

- Use tools like **Apollo.io**, **Hunter.io**, or **Crunchbase** to find recently funded startups
- Recently funded companies (Seed / Series A) often need to ship fast but haven't built out their team yet
- Send a short, personalized cold email similar to the LinkedIn templates above

## General Tips

- **Track everything** — use a spreadsheet or CRM to track who you messaged, when, and follow-ups
- **Follow up** — most people don't respond to the first message; a polite follow-up after 5-7 days works
- **Have a portfolio ready** — a simple personal site or a well-organized GitHub profile goes a long way
- **Be specific about what you offer** — "I build APIs and set up cloud infra" beats "I'm a full-stack developer"
- **Set your rate early** — know your hourly/weekly/project rate before conversations start
